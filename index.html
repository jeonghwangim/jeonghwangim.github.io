About

JH Gim (b. 1996, Seoul) is an artist and filmmaker based between Vienna and Seoul. He is currently studying at the Academy of Fine Arts Vienna, Art & Time | Film (2025– ), under Prof. Emily Wardill. His practice moves across moving image, performative installation, and sculptural arrangement.

His work examines how bodies and images are produced through technical and domestic arrangements: screens, furniture, sound, interfaces, systems of measurement, and the habits formed around them. Rather than treating the image as a representation of a body, Gim approaches it as a condition that reorganizes posture, attention, behavior, and belief.

Familiar materials — folding screens, furniture, mirrors, food, white-noise machines, projections, and infrastructural fragments — appear not as props but as active conditions. They shape atmosphere, choreograph movement, and expose the home as a studio, shrine, stage, surveillance device, and malfunctioning interface at once.

Across these works, dependency appears less as a stable relation of support than as a field of pressure: bodies, objects, technical systems, and non-human actors sustain one another while also producing coercion, habit, and quiet constraint.

Selected Works
near the well 2026
MediumVideo installation
ComponentsParavent (folding screen), projection, acrylic mirror, sound, white-noise machine
Duration7 min, HD
DimensionsFolding screen approx. 3 m wide, 10 cm deep
ContextVienna and Seoul

A seven-minute video installation projected onto a folding screen, doubled by an acrylic mirror and carried by the continuous presence of a white-noise machine. The work traces how a body becomes legible through images that name, simplify, condemn, and circulate it.

Rather than presenting perception as immediate, near the well stages it as a technical construction. Projection, reflection, furniture, and ambient sound form a perceptual apparatus in which the body appears only after being filtered through media, atmosphere, and display.

freekick, non-freekick 2025
MediumPerformative installation
ComponentsSculptural structures, moving image, interface graphics, sound
DimensionsRoom-scale, approx. 16 m²
Context1AO SPACE, Seoul
PerformanceGaeun Lee
CurationJinseo Park

Taking the EA FIFA video game series as a point of departure, freekick, non-freekick examines how football culture turns the body into a readable and tradable unit. Kick accuracy, sprint speed, player ratings, and interface graphics convert bodily presence into performance data.

The installation approaches this logic as an economy of comparison, competition, and metric control. Bodies and technical systems do not simply reflect one another; they actively produce one another through scores, displays, gestures, and repeated forms of spectatorship.

ohayo 2023
MediumSingle-channel video
PropertiesColor, sound
Duration8′44″
ContextSeoul / Mainz

A Korean family’s online worship is interrupted by an unknown error. From that interruption, the film drifts through blue skies, kebabs, and the hidden logic of a cactus. Positioned by the window, the plant observes the household and opens a passage into another dimension.

Domestic belief, interface failure, and more-than-human observation fold into one another. The home becomes shrine, screen, and malfunctioning system, while the plant is treated not as a decorative object but as a witness that changes how the scene can be perceived.

aftercrop 2026
MediumPerformative installation
ComponentsIKEA furniture, rice, wooden sculpture, floor lamp
Dimensions800 × 400 × 500 mm
ContextMainz, studio work

A compact sculptural arrangement of imported furniture, rice, worked wood, and domestic lighting. Drawers open onto grain; a lamp holds a cracked form; storage, offering, and collapse occupy the same provisional structure.

aftercrop reads the domestic as an imported system of use. Food, form, and infrastructure are received, held, and quietly deformed by a body that inhabits them without fully inheriting them. Interdependence appears here through ordinary objects that sustain survival while remaining fragile, unstable, and materially unresolved.

happymeal 2024
MediumMachinima installation
ComponentsIndustrial mesh net, projection, sound
DimensionsVariable, site-responsive
ContextUnderground parking garage

A machinima projection cast onto industrial safety mesh in a subterranean parking space. Chickens, pigs, and cattle move through rendered enclosures — barns, transport cages, feeding lines — while the mesh functions simultaneously as fence, filter, and screen.

Livestock appears not only as animal life but as logistical body: fed, tracked, weighed, and moved through infrastructures that make life inseparable from management. Concrete columns, artificial lighting, and ventilation extend the barn into the underside of the city. Interdependence appears as a supply chain in which care, capture, and consumption share one continuous surface.

Fans, Fans. 2026
MediumPerformative installation
ComponentsMirror-polished cylinders, white-noise machine, porcelain, smoke detector, wooden structures
DimensionsVariable, site-responsive
ContextAula Hall, Academy of Fine Arts Vienna
CurationYein Lee

Staged in the historic Aula of the Academy of Fine Arts Vienna, Fans, Fans. disperses mirror-polished cylinders, porcelain, a white-noise machine, a smoke detector, and wooden structures across a monumental floor. Reflection turns the institution back onto itself, while the machines produce a controlled atmosphere of technical calm.

Domestic fragments meet ceremonial architecture; comfort and surveillance operate on the same surface. The installation reads interdependence as infrastructural rather than harmonious: an ambience assembled from devices, objects, and institutional space, in which human presence is held, watched, and quietly managed.

The work can be adapted to the DAS WEISSE HAUS souterrain as a reduced constellation.
